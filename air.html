Air Pollution


What is Air Pollution?

Air pollution occurs when harmful substances, such as gases, particles, or biological molecules, are introduced into Earth's atmosphere. These pollutants can be natural (like volcanic eruptions) or human-made (such as emissions from vehicles and factories). Over time, air pollution negatively affects human health, damages ecosystems, and disrupts the planet's natural climate systems.

Causes
  • Factories: Release of harmful gases like sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides.
  • Vehicles: Emission of carbon monoxide and particulate matter from cars and trucks.
  • Burning Fossil Fuels: Coal, oil, and gas burning emit greenhouse gases like CO2.
Effects
  • Respiratory Diseases: Increases risks of asthma, bronchitis, and lung cancer.
  • Global Warming: Excess CO2 traps heat, leading to climate change.
  • Acid Rain: Sulfur and nitrogen oxides combine with water vapor, harming crops and buildings.
