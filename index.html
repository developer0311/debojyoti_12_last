What is Pollution?

Pollution refers to the introduction of harmful substances, contaminants, or energy (such as noise, heat, or light) into the natural environment. These pollutants can adversely impact the health of living organisms, disrupt ecosystems, and degrade the quality of air, water, and soil. Pollution can arise from natural events like volcanic eruptions or human activities, such as industrialization, deforestation, and urbanization.
