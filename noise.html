Noise Pollution


What is Noise Pollution?

Noise pollution refers to the excessive or harmful levels of noise in the environment, which disrupts the well-being of humans and animals. It often originates from urbanization, industrial activities, and transportation systems. Prolonged exposure to noise pollution can lead to physiological and psychological stress, as well as disturbances in ecosystems.

Causes
  • Traffic: Constant noise from honking, vehicle engines, and roadworks.
  • Industrial Activities: Machinery and equipment in factories generate high levels of noise.
  • Construction: Noise from construction sites, including drilling and heavy machinery.
  • Urbanization: Overcrowded cities with nightlife and daily hustle add to noise pollution.
Effects
  • Stress and Anxiety: Persistent noise increases stress and irritability.
  • Hearing Loss: Long-term exposure to loud noises can result in hearing impairment.
  • Impact on Wildlife: Disturbs animal communication, mating, and navigation.
  • Sleep Disturbance: Excessive noise interrupts sleep patterns, causing fatigue.
